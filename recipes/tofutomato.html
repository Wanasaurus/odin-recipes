Tofu stewed in tomato sauce

(Insert image here)

Description

I got inspiration for this dish from Vietnamese cuisine. There's a Vietnamese dish that consists of fried tofu cooked in tomato sauce. My version does not use fried tofu and contains Japanese mirin. This is a comforting dish that goes so well with rice.

Ingredients

This recipe serves four.

  • One block of firm tofu (you can use the silken kind, but then you will have to stir the tomato sauce carefully to prevent tofu breakage)
  • Two and a half large tomatoes
  • Five shallots
  • Small thumb or knob of ginger
  • Two cloves of garlic
  • One chilli padi
  • One tablespoon of fish sauce
  • One tablespoon of mirin (if you have dietary restrictions against alcohol, use some sugar instead)
  • Neutral flavoured cooking oil e.g. canola
  • Coriander to taste
  • Spring onion to taste

Steps

  1. Chop the shallots, ginger, garlic cloves, and chilli padi into small pieces.
  2. Slice firm tofu into small cubes.
  3. Dice the tomatoes.
  4. Chop coriander and spring onion and set aside. You'll use these when the stew is almost done cooking.
  5. Heat the oil in a pot and add the shallots. Fry until they start to caramelise (about 20 to 30 minutes).
  6. Add the chopped garlic, ginger, and chilli padi. Fry until fragrant.
  7. Add the tomatoes and stir. Put a lid on the pot. Let the tomatoes and other ingredients cook until they become a sauce (about 15 to 20 minutes).
  8. Put the tofu into the pot and coat well with the sauce.
  9. Mix the fish sauce and mirin in a bowl. Pour the mixture into the pot.
  10. Simmer the stew (with lid on) for about 15 to 20 minutes. Stir occassionally.
  11. Sprinkle the spring onion and coriander over the stew. Stir and let them cook for about a minute, or until fragrant.
  12. Serve with rice.
