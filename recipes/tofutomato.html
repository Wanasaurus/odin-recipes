Tofu stewed in tomato sauce

(Insert image here)

Description

I got inspiration for this dish from Vietnamese cuisine. There's a Vietnamese dish that consists of fried tofu cooked in tomato sauce. My version does not use fried tofu and has some non-Vietnamese ingredients. This is a comforting dish that goes so well with rice.

Ingredients

This recipe serves four.

  • Two blocks of firm tofu (you can use the silken kind, but then you will have to stir the tomato sauce carefully to prevent tofu breakage)
  • Four large tomatoes
  • Four shallots
  • Two cloves of garlic
  • One chilli padi
  • Two tablespoons of fish sauce
  • One tablespoon of mirin (omit if you have dietary restrictions against alcohol)
  • Neutral flavoured cooking oil e.g. canola
  • Pepper to taste
  • Coriander to taste
  • Spring onion to taste

Steps

  1. Chop the shallots, garlic cloves, and chilli padi into small pieces.
  2. Slice firm tofu into small cubes.
  3. Dice the tomatoes.
  4. Chop coriander and spring onion. Set these aside for garnishing.
  5. Heat the oil in a frying pan and add the shallots. Fry until they start to caramelise (about 20 to 30 minutes).
  6. Add the chopped garlic and chilli padi, and fry until fragrant.
  7. Add the tomatoes and stir. Put a lid on the pan. Let the tomatoes and other ingredients cook until they become a sauce (about 10 to 15 minutes).
  8. Put the tofu into the pan and mix into the sauce.
  9. Mix the fish sauce, mirin, and pepper in a bowl. Pour the mixture into the pan.
  10. Simmer the stew (with lid on) for about 15 to 20 minutes.
  11. Sprinkle the spring onion and coriander over the stew. Stir and let the garnishes cook for about a minute, or until fragrant.
  12. Serve with rice.
